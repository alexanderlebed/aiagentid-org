Identity Persistence Guarantee

This document formally declares the persistence guarantees of identity records within the Agent ID declarative identity infrastructure.

Persistence Principle

Once an identity record is declared and publicly anchored to the Agent ID registry root, its existence becomes structurally persistent.

Identity records cannot be retroactively invalidated as historical identity declarations.

Continuity Property

Agent identity references remain permanently resolvable through their canonical identity identifiers.

Status changes may occur, but identity reference continuity remains preserved.

Infrastructure Role

Agent ID infrastructure provides identity continuity anchoring, not behavioral validation.

Identity persistence enables structural referenceability across time, systems, and implementations.
