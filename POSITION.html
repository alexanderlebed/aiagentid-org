Position

This document defines the foundational position of aiagentid.org.

Role

I am the initiator of aiagentid.org — a conceptual infrastructure proposal for a global identity registry for artificial agents.

This is not a startup and not a product initiative. It is an attempt to define a minimal layer of existence for a new class of socio-technical actors.

Problem Framing

Artificial agents are rapidly becoming actors with real-world consequences: they recommend, decide, transact, and act across systems.

However, current infrastructures provide:

  • no persistent agent identity,
  • no accountability linkage,
  • no continuity across platforms.

This creates a structural gap between agency and responsibility.

Why Now

The problem is no longer hypothetical. Agent-based systems are already deployed in:

  • finance,
  • compliance,
  • logistics,
  • decision support,
  • automated negotiation.

Regulation is emerging faster than conceptual clarity. Identity is a prerequisite for any meaningful accountability.

Scope

The proposal is intentionally minimal:

  • identity only,
  • no governance,
  • no behavior control,
  • no ethics frameworks.

This layer is meant to be neutral infrastructure, not a normative system.

It answers only one question:

“What exists?” — not “What should exist?”

Core Principle

Agent ID does not regulate behavior.
It regulates attribution of existence.

Without attribution, responsibility is structurally impossible.

Current Status

  • Public domain specification published
  • Open critique and legitimation phase active
  • Terminology and scope stabilized
  • Community input invited

Funding Logic

Support is sought not for product development, but for:

  • research time,
  • conceptual clarification,
  • community coordination,
  • early standard formation.

The goal is to allow the work to proceed without being forced into premature commercialization.

Commercial implementations may emerge on top of this standard.

The standard itself remains neutral, non-exclusive, and open.
